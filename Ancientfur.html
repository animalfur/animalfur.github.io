Ancient Uses for Animal Fur

Other than felting, people had many strange uses for animal fur a long time ago. For example, some people thought that wearing
animal fur would give them a trait from that animal. A hunter might wear a lion's coat because he wanted to be stronger
or braver. Also, people thought that in their dreams, they actually became their animal and spoke to others of that
species. But this faith in fur was not a good thing. Soon, a law was passed that lower-class women,
like workers, could only wear fur of foxes, otters, or the fur of small burrowing animals. Higher-class women could wear
any fur they wanted, and rich people would often mortgage huge estates or patches of land to buy a fur coat for
their wife. Hunters would hunt small animals, like foxes, or more impressive ones, like a polar bear or a tiger.
Either way, they could make a lot of money. But hunters had one large problem. If there was a small hole or cut,
or even a small amount of blood, the price would lower for that particular piece. As a result, very experienced hunters learned to kill an animal
without touching its fur. That was one interesting use for animal fur a long time ago - as a coat.

It is possible to use any animal fiber to felt. Wool is the best because it is dense and hardens easily, but you can use dog hair
as well. Since it is finer, it takes longer, but it works in the end. For part of my core project, I felt dog hair.
