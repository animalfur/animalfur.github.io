How Needlefelting Works

How does needle felting work? All you can see while doing it is the needle poking the wool and the wool compressing. But on a
microscopic scale, wool has a lot of little scales on it. Felting needles have tiny notches in them that pull wool down, but not
back up. When you poke the wool with the felting needle, the wool fiber is pulled down, and the scales lock
together. This is why the wool gets harder as you poke it more. On an industrial scale, wool goes through large machines
with thousands of felting needles that poke the wool relentlessly. That felts wool extremely efficiently, and that is how
many felting companies make money.

Needle felting is a simple hobby with a long history of animal fur and science behind the mechanics of how it works. It is almost 8000
years old, more than 20 times the age of America. It is amazing in its simplicity. Other than felting, people had very strange
beliefs in animal fur. The way it works must have taken years for ancient people to understand and implement.
Felting is a very fascinating craft.
