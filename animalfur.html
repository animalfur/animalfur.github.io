Research

My most important piece of research is, of course, how to needle felt. To needle felt, you use a needle with barbs that pull wool down
but not up. To use this needle, you poke the wool over and over, and gradually, the wool will be forced down and not get pulled back up.
Since the wool stuck near the center, it gets more and more compressed, and the shape you want to make slowly gets harder and more defined. This makes
the wool harder and your project more refined. I used this research in all of my creations, as without this research, I could not have done
any of the felting. This is my first piece of research I used in my project.

My second most important part of research is how to code HTML. To learn this, I went to the library and checked out a book on it, HTML5 In
Easy Steps. It taught me all of the commands I needed to code this website, as well as the resources that I needed to write the code.
The most important commands I used were the command to make a paragraph, the commands to style the background and format the text. These
were important because formatting the text makes it easier to present, and the command to make a paragraph was very important, as at least 3/4 of my
website is paragraphs.

Another important piece of research was the research about adding links to the website.. The command is "a href="" and then your link. I put links onto
my website and made them go to other parts of the website. This was important because if I did not know this function, I would have one giant web
page that would be long and boring. This function makes my web page more presentable and makes it look better.

Also, I needed to know how to clean the dog hair, as my project revolves around dog hair. To clean the dog hair, you have to put it in a mixture of
dish soap and water, and throughly rinse it out. Washing the dog hair is important, because otherwise whatever would be disgusting and full of
germs. It also makes the dog hair better to felt, because you cannot felt with a needle that has dirt in it. For these reasons, the knowledge of cleaning the dog
hair was very important to do this project.

Finally, knowing how to publish a Google Photo and putting it onto the website was very important. I had a lot of trouble publishing it at first, and
when I tried to put them on, an X came up. This is because my website could not get the picture. Eventually, I figured it out, and now my pictures are on my website.
My videos were even harder, and they could not be put onto the website at all. Instead, I made buttons with links to my videos, and it opens a new tab when you click on them.
Figuring out how to upload media onto the website was another very important piece of research that helped my website.

Interview

These are some questions I asked my interviewee, Claire Impens. Claire Impens is a felting instructor at thetinthimble.com. She has 15 years of felting experience.
What were some early problems you had when you started felting?
She said that her creations were too fuzzy and not smooth enough.
What advice do you have that could help my felting creations?
She told me to experiment in size and try my own ideas.
What makes you an expert at felting? Do you have classes for kids?
She has 15 years of felting experience, and she has classes for all ages.
How do you prevent accidents?
She says to slow down and give the craft your full attention.
Do you have a favorite kit or brand of needles?
She likes felting needles from her buisness, as well as roving from livingfelt.com.
Do you think felting dog hair could work?
She said yes, any textile fur will work.
